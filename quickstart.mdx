## Reading Paths

Different readers will find different parts of this book most valuable. Choose your path:

### New to VC Funds

If you just joined a VC fund or are new to the venture capital industry:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start with Part 1">
    Read all three chapters sequentially to understand how VC works, analyze your specific fund, and avoid common mistakes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Identify Your Fund Type">
    Use Chapter 2 to determine your fund's stage, volume, and strategy - this shapes everything you'll build.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Plan Before Building">
    Apply Chapter 3's lessons to avoid jumping in too early and building the wrong thing.

</Steps>

## Common Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Should I build or buy software for my fund?">
    This depends on your fund's stage, resources, and specific needs. See:
    - [Common Mistakes, #2](/part-1-understanding-vc/common-mistakes#mistake-2-not-aligning-on-buy-vs-build-strategy)
    - Part 2: The VC Tech Stack for category-by-category guidance (Coming soon)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What technology stack should I use?">
    Part 3, Chapter 13 covers technology choices with VC-specific reasoning. The short answer: use boring, proven technology that AI coding tools understand well (TypeScript, Next.js, Postgres). (Coming soon)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I model VC data correctly?">
    Part 3, Chapter 15 covers the critical distinction between companies and deals, plus all the entity types you need to track. This is one of the most common areas where developers go wrong. (Coming soon)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What data providers should I use?">
    Part 3, Chapter 14 covers data providers by category (company data, people data, signals) with specific vendor recommendations and cost considerations. (Coming soon)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I handle data security and compliance?">
    Part 3, Chapter 21 covers what's actually sensitive, what audit requirements matter, and how to balance security with productivity. Also see [Common Mistakes, #5](/part-1-understanding-vc/common-mistakes#mistake-5-not-understanding-data-sensitivity-and-compliance).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
